So What Does This Mean?

DME Provider of Record — Key Implications

The four most critical industry signals translated into direct implications and action items for a DME provider of record.

🔒
Regulatory Impact
CMS Moratorium — What It Means for Your Network
HIGH IMPACT
CMS froze all new DMEPOS Medicare supplier enrollments Feb 27, 2026. Six months minimum.
Your existing subcontractor network is now a fixed asset. No new suppliers can enter Medicare — competitors can't expand easily, but neither can you onboard net-new providers.
Audit approved supplier roster for capacity gaps by geography
Strengthen contracts with top performers — they cannot be easily replaced
Flag any supplier whose accreditation is at risk — loss is permanent
💊
Market Signal
GLP-1 Surge Is Reshaping Your DME Order Mix
WATCH CLOSELY
GLP-1 prescriptions grew 300%+ since 2022, driving CGM demand up 34% YoY.
Physicians prescribing GLP-1 therapies are becoming your fastest-growing prescriber segment for CGM, wound care, and infusion equipment as comorbidities cascade downstream.
Ensure CGM suppliers have adequate inventory — backlog risk is real
Build endocrinology practice relationships — new high-volume referral source
Watch formulary changes as payers react to GLP-1 cost pressures
🏠
Member Trends
Cost Barriers Are Driving Returns — A Hidden Quality Risk
MEDIUM IMPACT
1 in 4 members skipped or returned prescribed DME in 2025 due to cost concerns.
Equipment returns create operational cost and quality metric risk. Payers tracking DME adherence as a quality measure will scrutinize POR performance directly.
Work with payers on cost-sharing levers for high-abandon categories
Build member education into fulfillment workflow — literacy gaps drive returns
Flag rural delivery delays as a separate access barrier from cost
📡
Technology Signal
Connected DME and RTM Codes Are Untapped Revenue
OPPORTUNITY
New RTM CPT codes now reimbursable. Connected DME device growth at +28% YoY.
The shift to connected devices creates a data layer that didn't exist before — real-time adherence, remote monitoring revenue, and quality documentation all now possible.
Identify which suppliers in your network offer connected device programs
Explore RTM reimbursement as an incremental revenue and quality lever
Frame connected DME as a member experience differentiator with payers
