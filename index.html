DME POR Intelligence Layer

The industry news — translated into direct implications and action items for a DME provider of record. What HME News, CMS.gov, and the trade press won't tell you.

🔒
Regulatory Impact
CMS Enrollment Moratorium — What It Means for Your Network
HIGH IMPACT
CMS froze all new DMEPOS Medicare supplier enrollments with no end date.
Your existing subcontractor network is now a fixed asset. No new suppliers can enter Medicare — meaning competitors cannot easily expand, but neither can you onboard net-new Medicare-eligible providers.
Audit your approved supplier roster for capacity gaps by geography and product category
Strengthen contracts with existing high-performing suppliers — they cannot be easily replaced
Flag any supplier whose accreditation is at risk — loss means permanent removal from your network
💊
Market Signal
GLP-1 Surge Is Quietly Reshaping Your DME Order Mix
WATCH CLOSELY
GLP-1 prescriptions grew 300%+ since 2022, driving CGM demand up 34% YoY.
The physicians prescribing GLP-1 therapies are becoming your fastest-growing prescriber segment for CGM, wound care, and infusion equipment as comorbidities are managed downstream.
Ensure CGM suppliers in your network have adequate inventory — backlog risk is real
Build relationships with endocrinology practices — a new high-volume referral source
Watch for formulary changes as payers react to GLP-1 cost pressures
📋
Payer Intelligence
Prior Auth Reform Is Both a Relief and a New Compliance Burden
MEDIUM IMPACT
CMS mandated 7-day standard PA turnaround for MA plans effective 2026.
Faster PA decisions mean faster fulfillment cycles — but MA plans will compensate by tightening documentation requirements. Expect stricter scrutiny, not just faster decisions.
Prepare for stricter documentation scrutiny as payers adjust to the 7-day mandate
Invest in electronic PA integration now — Jan 2027 deadline rewards early movers
Track denial rate changes by payer as new rules take effect mid-2026
🏠
Member Trends
Cost Barriers Are Driving Equipment Returns — A Hidden Quality Risk
MEDIUM IMPACT
1 in 4 members skipped or returned prescribed DME in 2025 due to cost concerns.
Equipment returns create operational cost and quality metric risk. Payers tracking DME adherence as a quality measure will scrutinize POR performance — your member outcomes are becoming a contract lever.
Work with payers to identify cost-sharing reduction levers for high-abandon categories
Build member education into your fulfillment workflow — literacy gaps drive returns
Flag rural delivery delays as a separate access barrier distinct from cost
🤝
Competitive Intelligence
PE Consolidation Is Concentrating Supplier Power — Strategic Risk
STRATEGIC WATCH
47 HME acquisitions in 2024 — PE-backed roll-ups creating large regional operators.
As independent suppliers are absorbed, your negotiating position weakens. Large PE-backed HMEs have leverage to negotiate favorable contract terms and may prioritize volume partners over smaller PORs.
Diversify your supplier network to avoid over-reliance on any single PE-backed operator
Monitor acquisition activity in your key geographies — coverage gaps emerge post-acquisition
Consider value-based contract structures that incentivize performance over pure price
📡
Technology Signal
Connected DME and RTM Codes Are an Untapped Revenue Layer
OPPORTUNITY
New RTM CPT codes now reimbursable. Connected DME device growth at +28% YoY.
The shift to connected devices creates a data layer that did not exist before — real-time adherence tracking, remote monitoring revenue, and quality metric documentation are all now possible through device data.
Identify which suppliers in your network are offering connected device programs
Explore RTM reimbursement pathways as an incremental revenue and quality improvement lever
Frame connected DME as a member experience differentiator with payer partners
