So What Does This Mean?

DME Provider of Record Intelligence Layer

The industry news translated into direct implications and action items for a DME provider of record. What the trade press won’t tell you.

🔒
Regulatory Impact
CMS Moratorium — What It Means for Your Network
HIGH IMPACT
CMS froze all new DMEPOS Medicare supplier enrollments with no end date.
Your existing subcontractor network is now a fixed asset. No new suppliers can enter Medicare — competitors cannot easily expand, but neither can you onboard net-new providers.
Audit your approved supplier roster for capacity gaps by geography
Strengthen contracts with top performers — they cannot be easily replaced
Flag any supplier whose accreditation is at risk — loss is permanent
💊
Market Signal
GLP-1 Surge Is Reshaping Your DME Order Mix
WATCH CLOSELY
GLP-1 prescriptions grew 300%+ since 2022, driving CGM demand up 34% YoY.
Physicians prescribing GLP-1 therapies are becoming your fastest-growing prescriber segment for CGM, wound care, and infusion equipment as comorbidities cascade downstream.
Ensure CGM suppliers have adequate inventory — backlog risk is real
Build relationships with endocrinology practices — new high-volume referrals
Watch formulary changes as payers react to GLP-1 cost pressures
📋
Payer Intelligence
Prior Auth Reform: Relief and New Compliance Burden
MEDIUM IMPACT
CMS mandated 7-day standard PA turnaround for MA plans effective 2026.
Faster PA means faster fulfillment cycles — but MA plans will tighten documentation requirements to compensate. Expect stricter scrutiny alongside faster decisions.
Prepare for stricter CMN documentation as payers adjust to 7-day mandate
Invest in electronic PA integration now — Jan 2027 rewards early movers
Track denial rate changes by payer as rules take effect mid-2026
🏠
Member Trends
Cost Barriers Are Driving Returns — A Hidden Quality Risk
MEDIUM IMPACT
1 in 4 members skipped or returned prescribed DME in 2025 due to cost concerns.
Equipment returns create operational cost and quality metric risk. Payers tracking DME adherence as a quality measure will scrutinize POR performance directly.
Work with payers to identify cost-sharing levers for high-abandon categories
Build member education into fulfillment workflow — literacy gaps drive returns
Flag rural delivery delays as a separate access barrier from cost
🤝
Competitive Intel
PE Consolidation Is Concentrating Supplier Power
STRATEGIC WATCH
47 HME acquisitions in 2024 — PE roll-ups creating large regional operators.
As independent suppliers are absorbed, your negotiating position weakens. Large PE-backed HMEs have leverage to set contract terms and may deprioritize smaller POR partners.
Diversify supplier network — avoid over-reliance on any PE-backed operator
Monitor acquisition activity in key geographies — coverage gaps emerge post-deal
Consider value-based contracts that incentivize performance over price
📡
Technology Signal
Connected DME and RTM Codes Are Untapped Revenue
OPPORTUNITY
New RTM CPT codes now reimbursable. Connected DME device growth at +28% YoY.
The shift to connected devices creates a data layer that didn't exist before — real-time adherence, remote monitoring revenue, and quality documentation all now possible.
Identify which suppliers in your network offer connected device programs
Explore RTM reimbursement as incremental revenue and quality improvement lever
Frame connected DME as a member experience differentiator with payers
